What does functional medicine mean to you? I have to say I haven’t heard about functional medicine before. That’s not because I haven’t had my fair share of medicines, on the contrary, I’ve taken a lot of medicine since I was young. From my childhood memory, I was easy to catch a cold and fever, nearly every time I went through the same process: taking antibiotics, which didn’t work and the situation got even worse, and I ended up with infusion. Even though I was a girl turning ten, I knew some mouthful jargons like amoxicillin and cephalosporin, and I was told to take medicines as soon as possible when I showed signs of sickness. Obviously the idea was totally off when I look back, yet this kind of practice of medicine is very common in my country. People don’t have time to wait for the body to recover by itself, so they choose overmedication when they catch a cold, experience throat pain, etc. And they believe the sooner they take medicine, the less possible it will get worse. In fact, the abuse of antibiotics doesn’t make us healthier, but weaker. Take me as an example, the frequent use of antibiotics has lowered me immunity, and made me much easier to be sick again. So what is functional medicine? Is it the same I had experienced? After reading articles and watching videos, I have learned that functional medicine is patient-centered medical healing, instead of looking at and treating health problems as isolated diseases, it treats individuals who may have bodily symptoms. That is way different from the traditional medicine I knew. People take traditional medicine and the symptoms go away, like antibiotics. But the real causes are not found, which may cause potential diseases or the real disease being neglected at the best treating time. The older I get, the more I am aware of my body. I can feel what is going on with it and I believe it works according to a precise system that adjusts itself. From my perspective, the goal of modern medicine is to find the real causes of sicknesses, to prevent potential diseases and to help the body get healthier in a natural way, instead of removing the symptoms only. So what functional medicine means to me is that it’s a healthier way to use drugs, a better medical solution and a healthier lifestyle. I hope it can be better developed and be accepted by more people, which may change the wrong way of using medicine as I went through before.
